SessionTrail — Photo Journal & GPX

A photography journal where the session — not the individual frame — is the atom. Plan a shoot, record it on the trail with background GPX, photo log and voice memos, reflect on what worked, and ship a one-page PDF shoot report. Built for serious shooters: landscape, astrophotography, wildlife, travel, editorial.

🌅 NOAA conditions on-device · 📍 Background GPX · 🌌 Astro mode · 🇨🇭 Built in Switzerland

Every NOAA calculation (sunrise, golden hour, blue hour, civil/nautical/astronomical twilight, moon phase, Milky Way galactic core) runs on your device — no network needed in the field. iCloud syncs metadata privately; heavy artifacts (GPX, voice memos, reference snaps, PDFs) stay on the recording device.

Perfect For

  • Landscape photographers planning multi-day trips around golden hour
  • Astrophotographers chasing the Milky Way galactic core
  • Wildlife photographers tracking long stalks and event coverage
  • Travel photographers organising multi-country trips with mixed currencies
  • Working pros logging editorial / commercial assignments
  • Anyone who keeps a written shoot journal today and wants it searchable

Key Features

  • Three-phase model: Plan → Shoot → Review → Archived. Each phase has its own surface; the journal preserves what was true at shoot time
  • NOAA conditions snapshot: Sunrise, sunset, golden / blue / civil / nautical / astronomical twilight, moon phase + rise/set, Milky Way galactic-core visibility — all on-device
  • Destination local time: Sun, moon and Milky Way times automatically render in the destination's wall clock, not your device's. A session in Tokyo viewed from Berlin shows Tokyo's sunrise — no mental conversion required
  • Multi-day breakdown: Per-day golden / blue hour computed live for every day in the planned window
  • Auto-recompute: Conditions refresh automatically when you tweak the date or location during the Plan phase, then freeze when you advance to Shoot
  • Background GPX recording: Three sampling modes — Sparse (5–30 min, GPS duty-cycled to save battery), Standard (15–60 s), Dense (1–10 s, for wildlife and events)
  • Multiple GPX tracks per session: One session can hold many recordings (morning hike, evening walk, dawn excursion). Each is renameable, individually shareable, with its own time range and point count
  • Day-chip strip on Shoot tab: Multi-day sessions filter photo log, voice memos, reference snaps and tracks per planned day — entry counts shown right on the chip
  • Out-of-window validation: When you log a frame whose timestamp lands outside the planned shoot window, SessionTrail asks before saving and offers to extend the session dates so the entry lands on the right day
  • Photo log per frame: Subject, lens, filter, frame number, and stabilization setup (handheld, tripod, monopod, gimbal, slider, drone)
  • Voice memos with titles: AAC into a per-session folder; playback, swipe-to-delete, transcription-ready
  • Reference imagery: PHPicker for inspiration before the shoot; quick reference snaps from the iPhone camera during the shoot
  • Pre-shoot checklist: Reusable templates so packing for an astro shoot doesn't start from scratch every time
  • Session templates: Save a complete plan (subject, target output, gear, checklist) as a reusable preset — apply it once and the new session is set up
  • GearGrove kit sync: Apply gear kits managed in our companion app GearGrove with one tap; lent / returned items propagate automatically via App Group
  • Gear kit with snapshot semantics: Your loadout survives renames in the source gear catalog — the journal records what you actually took
  • Scoped tags: subject:landscape, location:wallis, mood:moody, plus custom prefixes
  • Trips: Group multi-session journeys ("Iceland 2026", "Provence Lavender Week") with one currency, one window, and aggregated costs
  • Cost tracking: Estimated on Plan, actual on Review, with live variance. Trip-level incidentals (flights, hotel, car rental) separate from per-shoot expenses
  • 50+ currencies: Curated picker (CHF, EUR, USD, GBP, JPY, CLP, ARS, ISK, PEN, BTN…) plus an "Other…" entry that accepts any 3-letter ISO 4217 code
  • One-page PDF shoot report: Title, dates, location, plan, conditions, gear, reflection, rating, outcomes, costs, photo log table, GPX stats — ready to file, print, or attach to a competition entry
  • Multi-page Trip PDF: Cover page (name, dates, location, currency, cost aggregate) plus one concise page per member session — perfect for portfolio submissions and grant applications
  • Session bundle ZIP: Export the entire session (every GPX track, voice memo, reference snap and reference image, plus a session.json manifest) in a single archive for cold storage or handoff
  • GPX-only bundle: Lightweight ZIP of just the tracks (renamed in chronological order) plus a manifest with labels, time ranges and point counts
  • Session & Trip recap views: Beautiful one-page summaries you can share as PNG straight from the app — great for Instagram, story-style trip reports, or sending a teaser to a client
  • Astro appearance mode: Red on black to preserve dark adaptation under the Milky Way
  • Theme picker: Light, Dark, System, or Astro from Settings
  • First-launch onboarding: Quick tour of the three-phase model and the gear kit philosophy so newcomers don't bounce
  • In-app Help & FAQ: Settings → Help answers the same questions you'd email about, without leaving the app
  • Toast / banner error UI: Recording failures, voice-memo glitches, missing permissions, kit-sync hiccups — every failure surfaces visibly so nothing is silently lost
  • Reflection prompts: What worked, what didn't, lessons, conditions vs. expectation, free notes — five structured questions per session
  • Star rating + outcomes: 0–5 stars on the session, plus tagged outcomes (published, printed, submitted, archived, reshoot needed)
  • Active vs Archived sections: Closed journal entries collapse below active work, with a top-level filter for location / date range / trip
  • Delete confirmation: Swipe-to-delete asks first — no silent destruction of GPX, voice memos and the photo log
  • iCloud private sync: Metadata only; binaries stay on the recording device
  • EN + DE: Full localization for English and German, with photographer jargon ("Shooting", "Aufnahme", "goldene Stunde", "Milchstrasse")

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a session and a trip? A session is one shoot — possibly multi-day. A trip groups multiple sessions under one named container (e.g. "Iceland 2026") with a single currency and trip-level cost pots for incidentals like flights and hotel. Sessions can stay standalone forever; trips are optional grouping.
How does the currency get picked? Three-tier cascade: 1) if the session is in a trip, the trip's currency wins; 2) otherwise the base currency from Settings; 3) device locale as fallback. Changing the base currency in Settings only affects new sessions — old ones keep what they were created with, so a photographer who relocates doesn't have last year's CHF amounts silently re-interpreted as USD.
How does the conditions snapshot handle multi-day shoots? The stored snapshot freezes the start day (so the journal preserves what you actually planned around). Below it, a per-day breakdown shows golden hour and blue hour computed live for each calendar day in the planned window. No extra storage — recomputed on demand.
What's Astro mode? Settings → Appearance → Astro pairs a dark color scheme with red accents — the same trick astronomy observatories use to keep your dark-adapted vision intact at night. Pick it before you head out under the Milky Way.
How do I get the GPX track into PhotoMetaPro? On the Review tab, the Track section has a Share button. AirDrop the file to your Mac, or save it to iCloud Drive, then use PhotoMetaPro's Import button — the GPX format matches its expected import exactly.
Archive or delete a session — what's the difference? Archive moves the session to a separate section on the list — it's still there, fully editable, and you can unarchive any time. Delete is permanent: it removes the GPX track, voice memos, photo log and reference snaps along with the metadata. The list always asks to confirm before deleting.
How do I save battery during a long shoot? Pick Sparse sampling on the Shoot tab and set a long interval (5–30 min). The GPS sleeps between samples — at a 10-min interval that's roughly a 5% duty cycle, so battery lasts much longer than continuous tracking. Standard mode is the default for typical hikes; Dense is for wildlife / events where every step matters.
Does my data sync across devices? Metadata syncs via your private CloudKit database (sessions, trips, plans, reflections, costs, the photo log). Heavy artifacts (GPX file, voice memos, reference snaps, generated PDFs) stay on the device they were captured on for v1. Multi-device sync of those binaries is on the roadmap.
Why does the conditions snapshot freeze when I tap Start Shoot? Journal preservation. Once you've started shooting, the journal needs to remember the conditions you actually planned around — even if the calculator gets a bug-fix later or you tweak the date for some reason. During Plan it auto-recomputes; once you advance, it locks.
Can I have multiple sessions in the same trip? Yes — that's the whole point of trips. Open the trip from the suitcase button on the Sessions list, drill into the trip detail, and tap "Add session to trip" at the bottom to create a fresh session inside it. Currency and cost-rollup are handled automatically.
What happens to the data when I delete the app? iOS removes everything — local files (GPX, voice, reference snaps, PDFs) and the local copy of the SwiftData store. Your CloudKit data is preserved in your iCloud account; reinstalling and signing in gets the metadata back. Heavy artifacts captured on the deleted device are not recoverable in v1.
Why do sun and Milky Way times sometimes show a "destination local time" label? Because you're viewing a session whose location is in a different time zone than your device. Apple's date formatter defaults to the device clock — which gives nonsense like "Sunrise 23:11" when you're in Berlin checking a session in Beijing. SessionTrail captures the destination's IANA time zone when you geocode the location and renders all ephemeris in that wall clock instead, with a small disclosure label so you know what you're looking at.
Can I record more than one GPX track in a single session? Yes — multiple tracks per session is a Pro feature. A morning hike + an evening walk on the same shoot become two separate .gpx files inside the session, each renameable, each individually shareable, with its own time range and point count. The bundle export ZIP includes all of them, named by your label.
What's the difference between the session bundle and the GPX-only bundle? The full session bundle is a ZIP containing every GPX track, every voice memo, every reference snap and reference image, plus a session.json manifest with all the metadata. The GPX-only bundle is much smaller — just the .gpx files (renamed in chronological order) plus a tracks.json manifest with labels, time ranges and point counts. Both are Pro features.
Why did SessionTrail warn me that my photo log entry is "outside the planned session window"? Because the timestamp for the entry — usually "now" — falls outside the session's planned start / end. The day-chip filter on the Shoot tab would hide it on every planned day, while the recap card would still count it, leading to a confusing mismatch. The dialog offers three options: add anyway (and use All Days to find it), extend the session dates so the entry lands inside, or cancel.
How does the GearGrove kit sync work? SessionTrail and our companion gear-management app GearGrove share a private App Group. When you mark a kit as ready in GearGrove, SessionTrail can apply that kit to a session with one tap — the gear loadout is captured as a snapshot so future renames in GearGrove don't rewrite your journal. Lent / returned items propagate automatically. Both apps stay on-device; no third-party server is involved.
Can I share a session as an image? Yes — open the session and tap the recap icon in the top-right. The recap view is a one-page summary (header, costs, reflection, conditions, photo log) that you can share as a PNG to Instagram, Messages, or anywhere else. Trip recap is the same but spans multiple sessions. Both are Pro features.
What's the difference between the free version and SessionTrail Pro? The full Plan → Shoot → Review workflow is free, including the NOAA conditions snapshot, photo log with all fields, voice memos, reference snaps, single-track Standard-mode GPX recording, templates, iCloud private sync, Astro theme and full EN + DE localization. SessionTrail Pro unlocks the multi-day day-chip filter, Trips with cost tracking, per-session and Trip PDF reports, multiple GPX tracks per session, Sparse + Dense battery-aware sampling modes, GearGrove kit sync, session bundle ZIP exports, and PNG recap sharing. One-time unlock — no subscription.

Häufig gestellte Fragen

Was ist der Unterschied zwischen einem Shooting und einem Trip? Ein Shooting ist eine Aufnahme — ggf. mehrtägig. Ein Trip bündelt mehrere Shootings unter einem benannten Container (z. B. «Island 2026») mit einer einzigen Währung und Trip-übergreifenden Kostenposten für Flüge, Hotel etc. Shootings können dauerhaft Einzel bleiben; Trips sind optional.
Wie wird die Währung gewählt? Drei Stufen: 1) Ist das Shooting in einem Trip, gewinnt die Trip-Währung; 2) sonst die Basiswährung aus den Einstellungen; 3) Geräte-Locale als Fallback. Eine Änderung der Basiswährung gilt nur für neue Shootings — alte behalten ihre Originalwährung, sodass ein Fotograf, der umzieht, nicht plötzlich letzte Jahres CHF-Beträge stillschweigend als USD interpretiert sieht.
Wie funktioniert der Bedingungs-Snapshot bei mehrtägigen Shootings? Der gespeicherte Snapshot friert das Startdatum ein (so bleibt im Journal, womit du geplant hast). Darunter zeigt eine Tagesaufschlüsselung goldene und blaue Stunde live für jeden Kalendertag im Plan-Zeitraum. Kein zusätzlicher Speicher — On-Demand neu berechnet.
Was ist der Astro-Modus? Einstellungen → Erscheinungsbild → Astro kombiniert ein dunkles Schema mit roten Akzenten — derselbe Trick, den Sternwarten verwenden, um die Dunkeladaption nachts zu bewahren. Vor dem Aufbruch unter die Milchstrasse aktivieren.
Wie bringe ich den GPX-Track in PhotoMetaPro? Im Review-Tab hat die Track-Sektion einen Teilen-Button. Per AirDrop an deinen Mac schicken oder in iCloud Drive ablegen, dann in PhotoMetaPro über den Import-Button laden — das GPX-Format passt exakt.
Archivieren oder löschen — was ist der Unterschied? Archivieren verschiebt das Shooting in eine eigene Sektion — es bleibt vollständig erhalten und editierbar, du kannst es jederzeit aus dem Archiv holen. Löschen ist endgültig: GPX-Track, Sprachnotizen, Foto-Log und Referenzaufnahmen werden mit den Metadaten entfernt. Die Liste fragt vor jedem Löschen nach.
Wie spare ich Akku bei langen Shootings? Im Shoot-Tab Sparse-Sampling wählen und ein langes Intervall einstellen (5–30 min). Das GPS schläft zwischen den Aufzeichnungen — bei 10 min Intervall etwa 5% Duty Cycle, also viel länger Akku als kontinuierliches Tracking. Standard ist Default für typische Wanderungen; Dense für Wildlife / Events, wo jeder Schritt zählt.
Synchronisieren sich meine Daten zwischen Geräten? Metadaten synchronisieren über deine private CloudKit-Datenbank (Shootings, Trips, Pläne, Reflexionen, Kosten, Foto-Log). Schwere Artefakte (GPX-Datei, Sprachnotizen, Referenzaufnahmen, generierte PDFs) bleiben in v1 auf dem aufnehmenden Gerät. Multi-Device-Sync für Binärdaten ist auf der Roadmap.
Warum friert der Bedingungs-Snapshot ein, wenn ich «Start Shoot» tippe? Journal-Bewahrung. Sobald du mit dem Shooting begonnen hast, muss das Journal die Bedingungen festhalten, mit denen du tatsächlich geplant hast — auch wenn der Berechner später einen Bugfix bekommt oder du das Datum aus irgendeinem Grund änderst. Während der Plan-Phase berechnet er sich automatisch neu; nach dem Wechsel ist er gesperrt.
Kann ich mehrere Shootings im selben Trip haben? Ja — genau dafür sind Trips da. Öffne den Trip über den Koffer-Button in der Shooting-Liste, navigiere ins Trip-Detail und tippe unten auf «Shooting zum Trip hinzufügen», um eine neue Sitzung darin anzulegen. Währung und Kostenrollup werden automatisch verwaltet.
Was passiert mit den Daten, wenn ich die App lösche? iOS entfernt alles — lokale Dateien (GPX, Sprachnotizen, Referenzaufnahmen, PDFs) und die lokale Kopie der SwiftData-Datenbank. Deine CloudKit-Daten bleiben in deinem iCloud-Konto erhalten; nach Neuinstallation und Anmeldung sind die Metadaten zurück. Schwere Artefakte vom gelöschten Gerät sind in v1 nicht wiederherstellbar.
Warum erscheint manchmal das Label «Lokalzeit am Zielort» bei Sonnen- und Milchstrassen-Zeiten? Weil du ein Shooting betrachtest, dessen Ort in einer anderen Zeitzone liegt als dein Gerät. Apples Datumsformatierer nimmt standardmässig die Geräteuhr — das ergibt Unsinn wie «Sonnenaufgang 23:11», wenn du in Berlin ein Shooting in Peking prüfst. SessionTrail erfasst beim Geocoden des Ortes die IANA-Zeitzone des Zielorts und zeigt alle Ephemeriden in dieser Wanduhr, mit einem kleinen Hinweislabel, damit du weisst, was du siehst.
Kann ich mehr als einen GPX-Track in einem einzigen Shooting aufzeichnen? Ja — mehrere Tracks pro Shooting ist eine Pro-Funktion. Eine Morgenwanderung + ein Abendspaziergang am selben Tag werden zu zwei separaten .gpx-Dateien innerhalb des Shootings, jede umbenennbar, jede einzeln teilbar, mit eigenem Zeitfenster und Punktanzahl. Der Paket-Export-ZIP enthält alle, benannt nach deiner Bezeichnung.
Was ist der Unterschied zwischen Session-Paket und GPX-Only-Paket? Das vollständige Session-Paket ist ein ZIP mit jedem GPX-Track, jeder Sprachnotiz, jeder Referenzaufnahme und jedem Referenzbild plus einer session.json-Manifest-Datei mit allen Metadaten. Das GPX-Only-Paket ist viel kleiner — nur die .gpx-Dateien (chronologisch umbenannt) plus eine tracks.json mit Bezeichnungen, Zeitfenstern und Punktanzahlen. Beide sind Pro-Funktionen.
Warum hat SessionTrail mich gewarnt, dass mein Foto-Log-Eintrag «ausserhalb des geplanten Sessionzeitraums» liegt? Weil der Zeitstempel des Eintrags — meistens «jetzt» — ausserhalb des geplanten Anfangs / Endes der Session liegt. Der Tagesfilter im Shoot-Tab würde ihn an jedem geplanten Tag verbergen, während die Recap-Karte ihn weiter zählen würde — eine verwirrende Diskrepanz. Der Dialog bietet drei Optionen: trotzdem hinzufügen (und über Alle Tage finden), den Sessionzeitraum so erweitern, dass der Eintrag drinnen landet, oder abbrechen.
Wie funktioniert die GearGrove-Kit-Synchronisation? SessionTrail und unsere Companion-Gear-App GearGrove teilen sich eine private App Group. Wenn du in GearGrove ein Kit als bereit markierst, kann SessionTrail dieses Kit mit einem Tap auf ein Shooting anwenden — die Ausrüstungsliste wird als Snapshot festgehalten, sodass spätere Umbenennungen in GearGrove dein Journal nicht überschreiben. Geliehene / zurückgegebene Items propagieren automatisch. Beide Apps bleiben auf dem Gerät; kein Drittanbieter-Server beteiligt.
Kann ich ein Shooting als Bild teilen? Ja — öffne das Shooting und tippe auf das Recap-Symbol oben rechts. Die Recap-Ansicht ist eine einseitige Übersicht (Header, Kosten, Reflexion, Bedingungen, Foto-Log), die du als PNG nach Instagram, Nachrichten oder anderswo teilen kannst. Trip-Recap ist dasselbe, umfasst aber mehrere Shootings. Beide sind Pro-Funktionen.
Was ist der Unterschied zwischen der Gratis-Version und SessionTrail Pro? Der vollständige Plan → Shoot → Review-Workflow ist gratis, inklusive NOAA-Bedingungs-Snapshot, Foto-Log mit allen Feldern, Sprachnotizen, Referenzaufnahmen, Single-Track-GPX-Aufzeichnung im Standard-Modus, Vorlagen, iCloud-Privatsync, Astro-Theme und vollständiger EN- + DE-Lokalisierung. SessionTrail Pro schaltet den Mehrtages-Tagesfilter, Trips mit Kostenverfolgung, Pro-Shooting- und Trip-PDF-Berichte, mehrere GPX-Tracks pro Shooting, Sparse + Dense akku-schonende Sampling-Modi, GearGrove-Kit-Synchronisation, Session-Paket-ZIP-Exporte und PNG-Recap-Sharing frei. Einmaliges Freischalten — kein Abo.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Data Collection

SessionTrail does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on third-party servers. The only network endpoint we use is your own private iCloud database, which is governed entirely by Apple's privacy policy and is bound to your Apple ID.

What's Stored Where

  • iCloud private database (CloudKit): session metadata, plans, reflections, ratings, outcomes, costs, photo log entries, voice memo metadata, trip records, tags, templates, and conditions snapshots. This is your private data — visible only to you, on your devices.
  • Local device only: the GPX file (track.gpx), the voice memo audio (.m4a), reference capture images, and generated PDF shoot reports. Heavy artifacts stay on the recording device in v1.
  • UserDefaults (local only): appearance preference, base currency, archived-session counter, last-review-prompt version, onboarding seen flag.

No Analytics, No Trackers

SessionTrail does not include any third-party analytics, crash reporting, advertising or tracking SDKs. No Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no Firebase, nothing. Your usage patterns are not measured by us or anyone else.

No Account Required

SessionTrail uses your existing Apple ID for iCloud sync — there's no separate account, no email signup, no password to set. If you don't sign into iCloud, the app still works fully on a single device.

Permissions

  • Location: only when you tap "Use my location" or start a GPX recording. Background location is requested for continued recording when the screen is off; it stops when you tap Stop or "Wrap Up & Review".
  • Microphone: only when you start a voice memo recording. Audio is written locally; we never upload it.
  • Photo Library: only when you add reference images via PHPicker.
  • Camera: only when you take a quick reference snap.

Maps & Geocoding

We use Apple's MapKit (for the location picker and the GPX track preview) and Apple's CLGeocoder (for reverse-geocoding city + country from a coordinate). Both are governed by Apple's privacy policy. We never send your coordinates to any third-party server.

Notifications

SessionTrail registers for remote notifications to support CloudKit push (so changes from another device land quickly) but does not send marketing pushes. The only user-facing notification is the in-app review prompt after the third archived session.

Children's Privacy

SessionTrail is rated 4+ and does not knowingly collect data from anyone, regardless of age.
